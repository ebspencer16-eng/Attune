Getting started

Each partner completes two exercises independently, you don't see each other's answers while you're doing them. Once both partners have finished both exercises, your results unlock simultaneously. You'll see a side-by-side comparison of how you each answered, along with observations specific to your pairing. The whole thing takes about 25 minutes per person.

There is no deadline. Your partner can complete their exercises tomorrow, next month, or a year from now — and your results will unlock the moment they finish.

Methodology

Each person's Exercise 1 answers are scored across 10 communication dimensions (energy, expression, conflict style, closeness, and six others). From those scores, each person receives a 6-letter individual style code. The system then evaluates both partners' full score profiles against all 25 couple type signatures simultaneously — the type whose pattern best matches the couple's combined profile wins. No single axis dominates; the designation reflects the complete picture.

Each of the 10 communication dimensions is grounded in peer-reviewed relationship science. The conflict timing axis draws on Gottman & Levenson's demand-withdraw research (1992). The closeness axis draws on Bowlby's attachment theory and Mikulincer & Shaver's adult attachment research. The expressiveness axis draws on Pennebaker's emotional disclosure work. The needs directness axis draws on Markman and colleagues' PREP research. A full list of sources and the rationale for each axis is available in the methodology document below.
