One small note about registries

Most registry platforms mark items as "purchased" automatically when the purchase happens on their site. For items added from external stores — including Attune — you'll need to mark it as purchased yourself after the guest buys it. We'll email you the moment a gift is made, so it takes 10 seconds.

Questions couples ask

Is this a real gift, or just an online thing?
Both. You can register for the digital version ($139) — instant access the moment your guest buys it — or the physical gift box ($174), which includes everything in the digital plus a printed workbook and notepad shipped in a keepsake box. Some couples like having the box to open together after the wedding.
What do we actually receive?
Two exercises you each complete privately (about 25 minutes total), a shared results page that shows both of you how you each communicate and what you each expect, and a practical checklist for the first few months of married life — name changes, finances, household logistics. The physical version adds a printed 20-30 page workbook generated from your specific results.
When should we do it?
Whenever feels right. Some couples use it in the weeks before the wedding to surface expectations they haven't talked about. Others wait until a quiet moment after the honeymoon. You have access for as long as your account exists — there's no expiration.
Is this a therapy thing?
No. Attune is a self-guided assessment — not therapy, not counseling. It surfaces how you each see things and gives you language to talk about them. Think of it as a structured conversation starter, not a clinical tool.
What if we already own a lot of this — can we exchange it?
Gift cards are valid indefinitely and transferable, so you can pass along an unwanted Attune gift to another couple getting married. Or redeem it for the anniversary package later if the newlywed version doesn't fit.
We're not sure our guests will "get it."
The couples who've gifted it tell us the same thing: the friends and family closest to you already know you're the kind of people who'd want this. Registry guests who want to give something meaningful (and not another wine decanter) tend to pick Attune first.
